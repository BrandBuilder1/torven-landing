The mechanism · 6 steps · peer-reviewed

How red light at 655nm physically unchokes a hair follicle.

Each step below is published in peer-reviewed dermatology and photobiology journals. We've included the citations. This is not biohacker theory. It's the mechanism behind red light therapy research studied for decades.

01
The photon reaches the follicle.

Red light at 655nm penetrates 3 to 5mm into your scalp — exactly the depth where your hair follicles, dermal papilla, and bulge stem cells live. Visible light. No heat. No drug.

02
Cytochrome c oxidase absorbs it.

Cytochrome c oxidase is the enzyme in your mitochondria that produces cellular energy. It has a strong absorption peak at exactly 660nm — which is why devices in the clinical trials use something in the 650-680nm window.

Karu, 2010 — Photochem Photobiol Sci
03
Nitric oxide is knocked off.

In a stressed, DHT-damaged follicle, nitric oxide binds to cytochrome c oxidase and chokes it. The photon knocks NO off the enzyme — physically. The brake comes off. Electron transport resumes.

04
ATP comes back online.

With the brake released, your follicle's mitochondria start producing ATP — cellular energy — at normal rates again. Released NO also dilates the local blood vessels. Blood, oxygen, nutrients to the follicle: all up.

05
The dermal papilla wakes up.

The dermal papilla is the conductor of the hair cycle. PBM modulates Wnt/β-catenin signaling UP (pro-growth) and TGF-β/BMP signaling DOWN (the exact pathway DHT uses to kill follicles). The follicle stops getting the "die" signal.

Liu et al., 2025 — J. Photochem. Photobiol. B
06
Anagen phase is induced.

Bulge stem cells push the follicle out of telogen (resting) and back into anagen (growth). Miniaturized vellus follicles begin returning to terminal phenotype. This is hair regrowth — measurable in clinical trials as a 39% increase in terminal hair count.

Lanzafame et al., 2013 — Lasers Surg Med 45(8):487-95

Can I use TORVEN with minoxidil or finasteride? +
Yes. TORVEN stacks cleanly with both. Red light has zero systemic effects, no drug interactions, and works through a different mechanism. Combination therapy (PBM + finasteride + minoxidil) outperforms any single therapy in the trials. Many TORVEN users also use it to come off minoxidil — gradually tapering down as the hair count gain holds.
I'm fully bald in some spots. Will this regrow that? +
We'd rather lose a sale than mislead you. Fully fibrotic / dead follicles cannot be regrown by any non-surgical method — including PBM. If a zone has been completely smooth for several years, the follicles are likely gone. TORVEN works on follicles that are miniaturizing (producing thinner, finer, shorter hair). That includes a thinning crown, a receding hairline, diffuse thinning across the top — all of which still have follicles producing some hair. If your scalp shows no hair whatsoever in an area, set realistic expectations for that area.
Are there side effects? +
No serious adverse events have been reported in the major clinical trials for red light therapy in hair support. Across 50+ years of LLLT use, the safety profile is excellent. Minor reports: mild scalp dryness or itchiness in under 5% of subjects, resolved without intervention. Closed eyes during treatment is standard precaution.
